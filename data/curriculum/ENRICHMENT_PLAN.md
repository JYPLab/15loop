# Curriculum enrichment plan

The official 3,000 headwords are the coverage map, not the complete set of
learner-facing items. The middle-school curriculum allows up to 1,500 learned
words and recommends that at least 90% come from the official base list. 15Loop
therefore expands depth and usable expressions before adding many new
headwords.

## Beta expansion target

Start with about 300 expression units after deduplication:

- 100–140 communicative-function patterns for requests, suggestions, opinions,
  apologies, clarification, confirmation, and conversation repair
- 60–80 high-value phrasal-verb meanings
- 100–150 high-frequency collocations
- 15–25 limited everyday idioms whose meaning cannot be predicted from their
  component words

Irregular forms and important derivatives remain linked to their headword
instead of being counted as new vocabulary:

- all irregular forms for the selected 70–100 lemmas
- 80–120 derivatives with meaningful changes in meaning, part of speech, or
  pronunciation

## Selection rules

1. Cover the national curriculum communicative functions before adding
   decorative idioms.
2. Add a separate item only when knowing each component word is not enough to
   understand or produce the expression.
3. Group variants under one expression family.
4. Write original meanings, examples, distractors, and audio prompts.
5. Expand or replace items using learner error rate, response time, and
   transfer to new contexts.

## Required content fields

Each learner-facing word or expression needs:

- stable id and expression family
- curriculum tier and source basis
- Korean meaning and English definition
- pronunciation/audio form
- original example and Korean translation
- context cue and recall prompt
- sound, meaning, and context distractors
- content review status and reviewer
- license status

Do not copy textbook, EBS, publisher, or third-party corpus definitions,
examples, exercise wording, or list ordering. External lists can inform
selection, while 15Loop learner-facing content must be written and reviewed
separately.
